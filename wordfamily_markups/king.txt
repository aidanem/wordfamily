#### The name `Innogen`=eng-Latn first appears as the name of the King Pandrasus's daughter in the medieval British legendary history, "Brutus of Troy". The variant `Imogen`=eng-Latn first appears in Shakespeare's _Cymbeline_. Shakespeare also used the name `Innogen`=eng-Latn in early drafts of _Much Ado About Noting_ about 12 years earlier; adding weight to the possibility that he intended to use `Innogen`=eng-Latn, not `Imogen`=eng-Latn in _Cymbeline_.

So, the English name `Imogen`=eng-Latn is ultimately based on two compounded literary mistakes (probably).
