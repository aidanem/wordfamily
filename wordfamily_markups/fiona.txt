startnotes

# Welsh `pengwyn`=cym-Latn is probably actually rederived transparently in Welsh, rather than descended directy, but it seemed more organized to attach it here.

Likewise, Galician `Pena Oubiña`=glg-Latn (name of a mountain in Galicia in Northwest Spain) is likewise probably derived from a transparent phrase in Late Gaulish, and not from the Proto-Celtic personal name.

## Celtic <*Windobona>, tentatively derived as "white-base (of a hill)" or "white-village", was a settlement where Vienna would be. When the Romans built a fort there, they kept the name as Vindobona. The later Western Romance name may have been conflated with <*Vedunia>: "forest stream", before giving Italian `Vienna`=ita-Latn and French `Vienne`=fra-Latn.


### Finn MacCool, a legendary Irish hero, sometimes described as having prematurely white hair.
