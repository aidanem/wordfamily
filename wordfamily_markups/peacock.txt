# Latin `pavo`=lat-Latn: "peafowl" from Greek `ταώς`=grc-Grek <taṓs>, the `v`=lat-Latn is just an epenthetic glide between the <a> and <o>. The change from <t> to <p> was probably a modification by association with the Latin onomatopoeia for a peacock's cry `pau`=lat-Latn (`paupulare`=lat-Latn: "to cry, as of a peacock")

## The dance "the Pavane" is probably originally unrelated, most likely being a version of "Paduan", but it was later re-interpreted as meaning "peacock" resulting in the dance becoming more and more peacock-inspired.

### Hawaiʻian `pīkake`=haw-Latn refers to both peacocks and Arabian jasmine flower; both were imported around the same time and were associated with Princess Kaʻiulani (the heir apparent to Queen Liliʻuokalani at the time of the overthrow of the Kingdom Of Hawaiʻi).


#### Manchu `ᡨᠣᠵᡳᠨ`=mnc-Mong <tojin>: "peafowl, peacock" has to be a relatively recent borrowing, or the <t-j> sequence would have become <s-j> (cf. <sunja> from Proto-Tungusic <*tunga>: "five")

endnotes
