# Medieval Latin `foresta`=lat-Latn may be a blend of Latin `forestis silva`=lat-Latn: "the outside woods" and Frankish <*furhiþi>: "woodland, forest", from "fir (tree)" plus a collective placename suffix.


### Germanic has a distinction between <*durz>: "door, small door" and <*durã>: "big door, gate" (original a PIE collective noun) that is merged in most descendants, but the distinction can still be seen in German `Tür`=deu-Latn: "door, doorway" vs. `Tor`=deu-Latn: "gate, arch, goal".
