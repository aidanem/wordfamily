startnotes

# This is the origin of the shape of the letter <M>: a water ripple taking the sound of Phoenician <mm>. The hieroglyph of a single water ripple `𓈖`, which is probably the origin of Phoenician `𐤌`, was used in Egyptian for the sound /n/, acrophonic for a different word for water. The Sinaitic/Canaanite abjad gave it the sound of /m/ after the Canaanite <m(y)m>. The Greek letter shape and value are via Phoenician, but the name has the appearance of being direct from Egyptian.

## At least according to Herodotus, the ancient Persians wrapped their royal dead in wax or waxed cloth before internment (and after something related to dakhma furneral still practiced by the Parsis). That would fit well with the Persian word for "mummy" being derived from "wax".
