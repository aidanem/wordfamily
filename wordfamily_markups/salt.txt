## The exact reason why the Latin word `salÄrium`, etymologically "(thing) pertaining to salt" means "wages, salary" is unclear and much debated. It is probably not because Roman soldiers were literally paid in salt. It is more likely that "salt" stood metonymically either for miscellaneous supplies the soldiers paid for out of their wages; or else that "pertaining to salt" referred more literally to the military rations they were provided, and extended to the money they were paid as well (cf. the English use of `bread` to mean "money").
