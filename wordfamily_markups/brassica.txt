startintro

There's a very cool fact, that cabbage, broccoli, cauliflower, kale, Brussels sprouts, collard greens, kohlrabi, and others are all the species of plant: Brassica oleracea, selectively bred for different parts of the plant.
https://www.google.com/search?q=brassica+oleracea+selection

I had known that for many years before I noticed that many of the English words are all the same word, too: cauliflower, kale, collard, kohlrabi, coleslaw.

But conspicously not:
`broccoli`, despite the 'col' in the middle. `broccoli` is a plural dimunitive of `brocco`, just cannoli is a plural dimunitive of `canna`: "tube, cane" (see wff-cane). `brocco` is probably means "pointy", related to "brooch" and "brocade".
`kailan`/`gailan` (or "Chinese kale") which, despite the 'kail' sequence, is really kai (or gai) + lan, and ultimately from Old Chinese <*kreːds> + <*ɡ·raːn>
`cabbage`, of unknown origin, which is not explainable as some weird French version of `caulis`. Though it may be a weird French versions of `caput`: "head".
`Brussels sprouts`, which of course is named after the city of Brussels. But in Romance languages, `Brussels sprouts` is generally literally "cabbage of Brussels", and uses the Romance "cabbage' words in this family (e.g., `chou de Bruxelles`, `cavolino di Bruxelles`, `col de Bruselas`); and in many Germanic languages is literally "rose-cabbage", and likewise uses the version of this word, e.g. `rosenkål`, `Rosenkohl`, etc.


endintro
