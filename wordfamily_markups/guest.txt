This word for "guest"/"host" is restricted to the Late Northwest Indo-European languages, appearingâ€”as far as I knowâ€”only in Balto-Slavic, Germanic, and Italic, plus a single personal name in Lepontic (Celtic). It seems to be cognate to a word for "to eat" which is only attested in Indo-Iranian.

The word for guest and host appear to have been interchangeable in antiquity. Possibly because hospitality was viewed as reciprocal, and/or the original word may have simply meant "stranger that you are eating with", without specifying the guest or host roles.

Because it meant "stranger", it sometimes also came to mean "enemy, invader". So several contradictory English words all come from the same source.
