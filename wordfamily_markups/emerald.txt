startintro

Both Classical Sanskrit `𑀫𑀭𑀓𑀢` <marakata>: "emerald" and Classical Greek `μᾰ́ραγδος` <máragdos>: "emerald" appear to be borrowed from a common source that would have looked like <*maraktV>.

This is presumably a form closely related to the Phoenician <brqt> and Hebrew <baréket>: "emerald", literally "shining thing". The Akkadian cognate <baraqu>: "shine" might have had an equivalent  <*baraqtu(m)> which would be an excellent source for <*maraktV>, but is unattested afaik.


endintro
