## A large number of Egyptian consonants would become Coptic  `ϣ`=cop-Copt/<š> in this position, so it's impossible to reconstruct what it would have been. Honestly, there are several possibilities for the vowels, too.

# The Latin and Greek roots used in scientific English, Latin `fēlēs`=lat-Latn and Greek `ἴλουρος`=grc-Grek <aílouros>, are both primarily words from before domestic cats were introduced to those cultures, and the word "cattus" was introduced along with domestic cats. Similarly, the earlier Slavic <*mačьka>, was mostly replaced in East and West Slavic with introduction of domestic cats (but mostly retained in South Slavic).
