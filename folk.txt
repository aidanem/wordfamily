# Greek `πλείων` <pleíōn>: "more" and πλεῖστος <pleîstos> "most" are obivously related in some way Greek `πολῠ́ς` <polús>: "many", but I don't have the details of that derivation. 


## In Latinate words like `simple`, `double`, `triple` it is sometimes hard to know the origins of the `-ple`-type suffix, due to the confusability of Latin `plus`: "more" and `plex`: "fold". In many cases both words existed in Latin side-by-side (`simplus`/`simplex`, `duplus`/`duplex`, etc.) which under went some combination of merger or influence to give a single inherited word (often with later re-borrowings of one or both forms). 

This is probably mirrored in Greek <-plóos> and <-plásios>, which are both used to form adjectives from numerals and generally considered alternate forms of each other, but look like cognates of `plus` and `plex`, respectively. As in many things, the Latin may have been influenced by the Greek.
