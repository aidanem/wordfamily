##### <*rÄ«kijÃ£> and <*rÄ«kijaz> are a doublet pair in Germanic, both meaning "kingly". One borrowed with morphology applied in Celtic, one with the equivalent native morphology applied after borrowing from Celtic.

Germanic <*rÄ«kijaz> is the root of the French `riche`, Italian `ricco`, and Spanish `rico`, via the three Germanic peoples which took over each of those Roman provinces: the Franks, the Langobards, and the Visigoths, respectively.
