This is quite a large family with a number of borrowings into other language families. Because, it turns out, the Indo-European speakers' relationship with wheels is pretty important in a lot of people's history, like [wff-cane|the Sumerians' relationship with reeds].

There is also a root <*wel(hâ‚)->: "to turn, to wind, to eddy", which it's hard to argue is completely unrelated to <*kÊ·el(hâ‚)->: "to roll, to turn"! But they're both quite big families, and I couldn't manage to do them both at once. The <*wel(hâ‚)-> family includes words like `revolution`=eng-Latn, `waltz`=eng-Latn, `walk`=eng-Latn, `vulgar`=eng-Latn, and `wall`=eng-Latn. And then there's <*welhâ‚->: "to choose, to want", possibly "to turn towards"?, with words like `will`=eng-Latn and `voloptuous`=eng-Latn.
